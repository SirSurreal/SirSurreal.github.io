Hands on case study

 HTML is one of my favorite things to do and it's what I plan on doing for a career. I studied for two years at the Sanilac ISD and have the experience to show it. I've learned how to make what I want appear on the screen, where, and how I want it. On this part of my site I show my progress over the two years. Starting off with my first hands-on activities, where I copied from the book how to style web elements in a certain way and what all the tags mean and can do. I then took it a step further and enjoyed playing with the code to see what I, and the code, can do.


Optical Illusion Art Project

 The Optical Illusion Art Project was an assignment given to me in which I had to create a 5 page website based of an art movement from a list. I settled on OP art, or Optical illusion art. This website I enjoyed making partly from learning interesting things about this genre of art, but also from learning more techniques for designing and the difficulty of positioning divs.





Dayz Project

 This project was my final for my first year of HTML. I was given the directions of making a website about a game that you enjoy. The four page site had some specific requirements such as a meter bar to measure anything we wanted, multiple gradients, multiple images, and an unordered list. Even with these requirements we were given creative freedom to do code however we wanted. This was a first for us to be able to have the freedom to make it how we saw fit.
